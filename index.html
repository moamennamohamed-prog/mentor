The 5-Part Structure
1. Specific Opener
Never start with "Hope this finds you well." Reference something REAL: a post they shared, a project on their GitHub. Specific = credible.
2. One-line Identity
Who you are in one line relevant to THEM. "I'm a Junior Data Engineer who recently built an Airflow pipeline for..." Not your whole CV.
3. One Proof Point
One specific, relevant fact. A project, a result, a tool. Pick the most relevant one for THIS person — not a list.
4. Low-Friction Ask
Never ask for a job in message one. Ask for a 15-min chat, feedback, advice. These get yes far more often.
5. Easy Exit
"No pressure at all" or "Totally understand if you're busy" makes the ask feel safe to say yes to.
⚠ Common Mistakes
  • Sending the exact same message to 50 people
  • Opening with generic flattery instead of something real
  • Writing more than 100 words
  • Asking for a job in the very first message
  • Following up more than once without a new reason
  • Messaging only senior leaders instead of ICs and recruiters
Cold LinkedIn Outreach
Hi [Name], I saw your post about [specific thing] — really resonated with [reason].

I'm a Junior Data Engineer who recently built [specific project] using [tool]. I'm exploring opportunities at [Company] and would love to hear about your experience on the data team, if you have 10-15 minutes.

No pressure at all — happy to work around your schedule.
· Reference something real and specific — never generic · Under 80 words · Ask for a conversation, not a job
Application Follow-Up (after 7 days)
Hi [Name], I applied for the [Role] on [Date] and wanted to follow up. I'm still very interested — I built [specific project] which I think aligns well with what your team works on.

Happy to share more or jump on a quick call. Thanks either way.
· Follow up once only · Reference your specific fit · Keep it short
Informational Interview Request
Hi [Name], I've been following your work in data engineering and would love to learn from your experience. Would you be open to a 15-minute chat about your path into the field?

I'm happy to work around your schedule. Totally understand if you're too busy.
· Propose specific time windows · Don't ask for referral in first message · Always follow up with a thank-you
Thank-You After a Call
Hi [Name], thank you for the time today — I really enjoyed learning about [specific thing discussed]. It gave me a clearer picture of [specific takeaway].

Thanks again, and I hope we stay in touch.
· Send within 24 hours · Mention one specific detail from the conversation · Brief — not another pitch
